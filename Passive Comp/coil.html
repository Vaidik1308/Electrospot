Electric Coil

What is an Electric coli?

An electric coil, or electromagnetic coil, is an electrical conductor that contains a series of conductive wires wrapped around a ferromagnetic core that is cylindrical, toroidal, or disk-like. Electric coils are one of the simplest forms of electronic components and provide inductance in an electrical circuit, an electrical characteristic that opposes the flow of current.

There are a wide range of electrical coils that vary according to the gauge of their wires, coil diameter, wire lengths, the number of loops, and type of core material, which can be air, iron, steel, ceramic material, or iron based formless tape. The materials used for the core of an electric coil is determined by the permeability of the material, which is the core's ability to form a magnetic field.
